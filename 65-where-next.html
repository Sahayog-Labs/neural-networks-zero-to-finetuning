Converged in architecture, then diverged in economics

Architecturally, the story closed on the last page: FLUX.2's text encoder is a 40-layer Mistral-3 transformer — GQA, RoPE, a KV cache, the same block you spent Part V learning to fine-tune. Economically, 2026 split the two fields, and this is the part almost nobody predicts correctly:

🔑 Key idea — draw this deliberately (D-17) LLMs are still scaling. Image models are consolidating. The evidence, all VP (constants §9.6):
  • Z-Image-Turbo — 6B, 8 NFE, Apache-2.0 — is #1 open-weights on the AA Image Arena, above FLUX.2-dev, a 32B model.
  • FLUX.2-klein-4B runs 4 steps at guidance 1.0 in ~13 GB — a model you could run comfortably on a laptop GPU.
A 6B model beat a 32B model, and a 4B model runs on a phone-class budget — in the same year the LLM world was still stacking parameters (Llama-3.3-70B, the reference point three pages taught you to QLoRA, is bigger than either FLUX.2 point). Converged in architecture, diverged in economics. That is a better answer than "everything is one thing now" — and it's the honest resolution of the predict box above: image models, at the frontier, are trending smaller and cheaper per unit of quality, exactly backwards from how the LLM story went. If your bet was "bigger," you're not alone — it's the naive extrapolation from three years of LLM scaling, and it's the one the 2026 data overturns.

The tooling, honestly

What you'd actually reach for next, with the caveats a course that respects your time gives you up front (constants §7, hardware-ground-truth §3):

📐 The honest tooling map
  • torchtune is wound down. The best-documented DGX Spark fine-tune in existence — Llama-3.1-8B, seq 16,384, ~8 h/epoch, ~80% of 128 GB — runs on a library that is no longer actively maintained VP. torchforge is not a drop-in successor: it targets RL/agentic workloads and is explicitly experimental. Build nothing new on torchtune.
  • vLLM works on sm_121 — but the validated path is the container vllm/vllm-openai:cu130-nightly, pinned by digest. The PyPI aarch64 wheel is built against CUDA 12 and fails on the Spark's CUDA-13-only stack.
  • bitsandbytes ships native sm_121 only into the aarch64+CUDA-13 wheel — the Spark is better served here than an x86 box. This is what "Add release for DGX Spark" in its build script means, and it's the opposite of the "aarch64 is painful" framing you'd expect.
  • The real trap is narrower than "aarch64 is hard": any package pinning libcudart.so.12 fails at import on CUDA-13-only DGX OS, before kernel compatibility is even evaluated.

Pin versions (constants §7): torch==2.13.0 · transformers==5.14.1 · peft==0.19.1 · trl>=1.8,<2 · diffusers==0.39.0 · bitsandbytes==0.49.2. And install into a fresh venv — never ComfyUI's. His installed stack is ComfyUI's venv only (torch 2.12.1+cu130, no peft/trl/accelerate/bitsandbytes anywhere); the LLM stack is a second, separate venv, always.
